Define risk management in a business context.
Back:
In business, **risk management** is the process of **identifying**, **monitoring** and **managing** potential **risks** to minimize negative impact. Examples: **security breaches**, **data loss**, **cyber attacks**, **system failures**, **natural disasters**.

What are the main differences between ISO 27001:2013 and ISO 27001:2022?
Back:
- **Context Scope**: Must **identify relevant interested parties requirements**. ISMS explicitly includes **processes and interactions**
- **Planning**: Objectives **monitored as documented info**. New section for **planning ISMS changes**
- **Support**: Shift to **how to communicate** instead of who/processes
- **Operation**: **Process criteria for Clause 6 actions**. Control **externally provided processes/services**
- **Performance Eval**: Methods must be **comparable/reproducible**. Management review includes **parties changing needs**

**Annex A Controls**: **93 revised controls** aligned with ISO 27002:2022 (vs 114 in 2013)

What are the differences between qualitative and quantitative risk assessment?
Back:
- **Qualitative**: uses **scale of attributes** (**low, medium, high**) for consequences and likelihood. **Useful for initial screening**, **easy to understand**, but **subjective**.
- **Quantitative**: uses **numerical values** for consequences/likelihood (**historical data**). **More precise**, but **lacks data for new risks**.

Basic
Quantitative formula for Risk R?
Back:
**R = T × Im × ARO × V**
Where:
- **T**: threat probability (0-1)
- **Im**: impact (0-10)
- **ARO**: Annual Rate of Occurrence (0-1)
- **V**: asset value CIA (1-3).

What is NRV (Normalised Risk Value)?
Back:
**NRV = R / R_max** (R_max = max possible risk). Used to **prioritize** (e.g., **NRV < 0.20** = accept/retain).

What is Residual Risk?
Back:
**Residual risk** is a **risk that remains** after all the **Risk Management options** have been **identified and action plans implemented**.
Includes:
- **all initially unidentified risks**
- **all risks previously identified and evaluated** but not designated for treatment
Must be:
- **documented**
- **subjected to regular monitor-and-review procedures**
- **well informed** organization's management and decision makers.
